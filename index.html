The Problem

Pressure injuries remain one of healthcare’s most preventable complications.

Patients who remain seated or bedridden for extended periods experience sustained tissue compression between bone and external surfaces. Over time, prolonged pressure restricts circulation, increases localized stress accumulation, and elevates the risk of tissue breakdown. Existing prevention systems are frequently expensive, inaccessible, or reactive rather than predictive.

Pressure + Duration

Pressure injuries are influenced not only by force magnitude, but also by how long tissue remains compressed without relief. Continuous exposure over time significantly increases injury risk.

Accessibility Gap

Advanced monitoring systems used in clinical environments can cost thousands of dollars, limiting adoption in underfunded hospitals, rehabilitation facilities, and home healthcare settings.
